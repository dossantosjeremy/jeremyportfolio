# What Does a Courier Do When Not Finding the Customer?

> **One-line hook**: *"In Nairobi, sometimes I prefer to use the incorrect address contact option to save my life."* — Couriers weren't avoiding Glovo's customer-absent automation out of laziness. They were avoiding it strategically. Understanding why unlocked a set of design changes with direct impact on courier safety and support costs.

## Background & Problem Statement

When a customer is unreachable during a delivery, Glovo had an automated "customer absent" feature. The problem: couriers were systematically avoiding it — choosing instead to contact support via the "incorrect address" option, which triggered a more expensive human agent contact.

**The research question:** What are couriers' thinking process and expectations when the customer is not reachable?

![The customer absent automation — the feature couriers were systematically avoiding in favour of the more expensive 'incorrect address' support path.](images/glovo-customer-absent-problem-visual.png)
*The customer absent automation — the feature couriers were systematically avoiding in favour of the more expensive 'incorrect address' support path.*

**My hypothesis:** By decreasing the customer-absent timer, couriers would use the automation more often when appropriate.

But before testing the hypothesis, I needed to understand the mental model — including why the incorrect address workaround was so prevalent.

## Research Approach

**Knowledge board first.** I began by structuring what we knew, what we thought we knew, and what we didn't know — to focus the research on the genuine unknowns rather than confirming existing assumptions.

![Knowledge board — structured to distinguish confirmed knowledge from assumptions before designing the research.](images/glovo-customer-absent-knowledge-board.png)

**Desk research and chat audits.** A striking finding emerged early: couriers were deliberately misclassifying their support reason — using "incorrect address" to avoid the customer-absent flow. This wasn't a mistake. It was a workaround. They had specific reasons for it.

**10 moderated interviews (1 hour each)** to explore couriers' mental models when the customer is absent. Participants included couriers from multiple markets.

## Key Finding: Strategic Avoidance

> *"Couriers avoid the customer absent automation because they aim at delivering fast and avoid cancellations."*

![Affinity diagram — 10 interviews synthesised into themes: fear of cancellation, timer friction, safety concerns, and lack of revert option.](images/glovo-customer-absent-affinity-diagram.png)
*Affinity diagram — 10 interviews synthesised into themes: fear of cancellation, timer friction, safety concerns, and lack of revert option.*

Four specific pain points drove the avoidance:

1. **Fear of cancellation:** If the order is cancelled, couriers feel obliged to take it back to the store — which costs them time and earnings
2. **Timer too long:** The waiting period before the courier can trigger the cancellation was perceived as excessive
3. **No revert option:** If the customer finally showed up after the process started, there was no way to continue the delivery
4. **Safety concerns:** Couriers in certain markets (Kenya surfaced most sharply) were waiting in areas they perceived as dangerous — and the wait time was a direct safety risk

> *"In Nairobi, sometimes I prefer to use the incorrect address contact option to save my life"* — a courier in Kenya

The safety finding was qualitatively significant and changed the priority order of the recommendations.

---

## UX Recommendations

Four concrete changes to increase use of the automation and improve courier peace of mind:

1. **Shorten the timer** — the wait before the courier can trigger cancellation is too long and drives the "incorrect address" workaround
2. **Add a revert option** — if the customer shows up after the process has started, the courier should be able to continue the delivery without restarting from scratch
3. **Communicate phone status** — tell couriers whether the customer is answering their phone, so they aren't waiting in the dark
4. **Safety redirect** — in markets or situations with safety risk, redirect the courier to a support agent rather than making them wait

## Impact

These recommendations provided the product team with a clear, evidence-based set of design priorities for the customer-absent feature redesign.

![UX Research Report — full findings and recommendations delivered to the Delivery Solutions product team.](images/glovo-customer-absent-research-report.png)
