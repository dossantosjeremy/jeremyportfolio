# Glovo — Courier Support Experience Redesign
### Slide / PDF Deck Version

## SLIDE 1 — Hook

**Couriers weren't using support wrong — the support section was built around the wrong mental models.**

Three interlocked research projects identified why, rebuilt the architecture to match how couriers actually think, and validated a 58% improvement in findability — reducing support escalation by 23%.

## SLIDE 3 — The Problem

**The business problem:** Contact ratio — every support ticket handled by a human agent costs money. Reducing self-service failure was the primary KPI.

**The user problem:** Couriers selected whatever option got them to an agent fastest, not the option that matched their issue.

> *"I would prefer if we had to click only on a few buttons and not have to click 2 or 3 times."* — Courier, Morocco

**Root cause (hypothesis before research):** The support IA was built around Glovo's internal taxonomy, not couriers' mental models.

**Root cause (confirmed):** Couriers categorise issues by **frequency, severity, and delivery phase** (pick-up vs. drop-off vs. earnings). The existing IA ignored delivery phase entirely.

## SLIDE 5 — Approach

**Project 1 — Discovering the mental model gap**

Method: Card sorting — externalises how couriers naturally group their issues, without leading them toward Glovo's existing categories.

Key finding: Three dimensions matter to couriers: frequency, severity, delivery phase. The existing IA covered only one.

**Heuristic analysis identified immediate issues:**
- First layer too long (overwhelming)
- No icons — non-fluent couriers had no visual anchors
- Critical options (accidents) placed next to frequent options → slips

**Project 2 — Earnings as a product, not a support ticket**

Prototype tested: Earnings estimator + adjustment request flow
Finding: Useful, but patronising — the explanation came *before* the option to request a review (reversed in recommendations)
Unexpected impact: Research elevated earnings to a dedicated Fintech product feature

**Project 3 — Quantitative proof**

284 couriers · 6 tasks · Unmoderated tree test
Compared old vs. new IA on: Success rate · Directness · Time · Path analysis

## SLIDE 7 — Recommendations Delivered

**For the IA redesign:**
- ⭐ Multi-route access: group content so couriers can reach the same resolution in multiple ways
- ⭐ Iterate copy: use courier-familiar vocabulary (not internal Glovo terms)
- ⭐ Remove irrelevant first-layer options; move critical options (accidents) to end of first layer
- ⭐ Add direct contact shortcuts when a courier selects a contact reason

**For Earnings Education:**
- ⭐ Allow request for review *first*, explanation *second* — remove the patronising framing
- ⭐ Display expected route beforehand for transparency
- ⭐ Increase findability of the KM estimator across multiple entry points

## SLIDE 8 — Impact

**Operational:** −23% support escalation rate (direct contact ratio KPI impact)

**Product:** Earnings transparency elevated from support feature to dedicated Fintech product — a strategic expansion far beyond the original brief

**Process:** Created a repeatable research-to-product cycle that linked each study to the next iteration — not a one-off engagement

---

## SLIDE 9 — Learnings

**What I'd repeat:** Embedding a heuristic analysis before every user study — it sharpens hypotheses, reduces wasted session time, and gives design teams immediate, actionable quick wins before the full research report lands.

**What I'd improve:** Add a contextual inquiry component — testing couriers mid-delivery, not just in Zoom sessions — to capture real-world friction that remote testing misses.

**What this project taught me:** Framing research around a single business metric (contact ratio) from day one was the key to PM trust and recommendation adoption speed. Research that speaks in product language moves faster.
