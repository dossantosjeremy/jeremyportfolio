## SLIDE 3 — The Problem

**The challenge:** Renault was building AI-enhanced vehicles that behave in "living" ways — but had no validated framework for understanding whether users would embrace or reject those characteristics.

**The risk:** Invest in features users will find intrusive or uncanny; or avoid features users would genuinely value.

**Primary user concerns (from initial survey):**
- *Intrusive behaviours* — 12% of unprompted responses
- *Unwanted living traits* — 6%
- But: *Life facilitation* was the 3rd most common AI association (10%)

> The gap between user openness and user anxiety was the research space to navigate.

## SLIDE 5 — Approach

**Phase structure:**

| Phase | Focus | Method |
|---|---|---|
| GEN1 Phase 1 | Storyboard evaluation of 5 AI scenarios | Online survey, Likert + open-ended |
| GEN1 V2 | Physical Firefly design expert review | Expert panel evaluation |
| GEN3 | Emotional AI responsiveness | Extended attachment model testing |

**The Firefly metaphor:** Subtle, bioluminescence-inspired light behaviours used consistently across all scenarios as the embodiment of AI's living characteristics — perceived as non-threatening and contextually appropriate.

**Theoretical framework (what we measured):**
AI Emotion · AI Adaptation · User Control · AI Memory · Context Sharing

---

## SLIDE 6 — Key Findings

**Regression analysis — predictors of usage intention:**

| Driver | β | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Perceived Usefulness** | **0.49** | p<0.0001 ★ |
| Attractiveness | 0.27 | p<0.01 |
| Adaptability | 0.16 | p<0.05 |

**Scenario ranking:**
1. 🥇 Intelligent Exit Assistance — highest acceptance (utility + living character integration)
2. 🥈 Vehicle Approach & Recognition
3. 🥈 Ambient Cabin Lighting
4. ⚠️ Pre-Drive Check — mixed utility/attachment balance
5. ⚠️ Dynamic Driving Response — mixed acceptance of living traits

**Key insight:** Utility is the gateway to acceptance. Living characteristics amplify engagement once utility is established — but cannot substitute for it.

---

## SLIDE 7 — Design Principles Delivered

Five principles codified as Alliance AI development standards:

1. **Utility first** — Functional benefit must be the primary proposition; avoid features without clear user utility
2. **User control is non-negotiable** — Customisation and manual override are acceptance-critical, not optional extras
3. **Context adaptation** — AI behaviour must adapt to usage context (urban/rural, experienced/novice)
4. **Progressive disclosure** — Introduce living characteristics gradually; avoid overwhelming first-time users
5. **Safety and reliability** — Fail-safes and clear system status are prerequisites, not enhancements

## SLIDE 8 — Impact

**Research impact:**

**Product impact:**
- Low-utility features deprioritised before design investment (resource saving)
- Firefly metaphor advanced to physical design prototype (GEN1 V2)
- Design principles adopted as Alliance-level internal standards

**Scale of influence:**
- Cross-brand impact: Renault, Nissan, Mitsubishi
- R&D, design, and brand strategy teams aligned

---

## SLIDE 9 — Learnings

**What I'd repeat:** Building the theoretical framework first was essential. It gave the research a structured vocabulary that design and engineering teams could actually use.

**What I'd improve:** The internal participant pool (Renault employees) limits consumer generalisability. A consumer study across key Alliance markets would complete the picture.

**Open question:** How does attachment to living AI characteristics evolve with long-term vehicle ownership — does familiarity deepen it, or does it habituate into invisibility?
