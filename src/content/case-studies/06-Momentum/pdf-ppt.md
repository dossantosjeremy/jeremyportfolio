## SLIDE 1 — Hook

**Most goal-setting apps fail at the same moment: the gap between "I want to do this" and "here's what you do tomorrow."**

I led the UX research for Momentum — an AI-powered app that bridges that gap by turning any life goal into a personalised, habit-driven plan.

## SLIDE 3 — The Problem

**Self-learners don't fail because they lack ambition. They fail because no tool bridges the gap between goal and action.**

| Pain point | User quote |
|---|---|
| No clear next steps | *"I set the goal but I never know what to actually do next"* |
| Abandoned tracking | Goals abandoned within 2–4 weeks — not from lack of motivation, but lack of structure |
| App overwhelm | Tools are either too simple (checklists) or too complex (framework-heavy) |
| No personalisation | Generic plans that don't adapt to the user's time, level, or motivation |

**The design opportunity:** An AI coach that personalises the journey from dream to daily habit — in plain language.

---

## SLIDE 4 — What Momentum Does

**Four-stage model:**

```
Dream input
  → AI dialogue: translate to SMART goal
    → Structured plan: milestones + habits + schedule
      → Daily accountability: check-in, adapt, celebrate
```

The AI functions as a coach: it asks questions, refines the goal, builds the plan, and adapts when life gets in the way.

**Behavioural science baked in:** habit stacking, implementation intentions, OKR framework (hidden from the user), four laws of behaviour change.

## SLIDE 5 — Research Approach

**Two-track research programme:**

| Track | Purpose | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | Validate concept with non-users | Product-agnostic interviews (ES/FR/UK) |
| Validation | Test prototype usability | Moderated think-aloud sessions |

**+ Competitive analysis:** Atoms, Dreamfora, Habitica, Fabulous, Notion templates

**Competitive gap confirmed:** No app combines AI personalisation + habit science + accessible language at the depth Momentum proposed.

---

## SLIDE 6 — Key Finding 1: Language is the onboarding killer

**Technical jargon created immediate drop-off.**

Terms like *"OKRs"*, *"milestones"*, *"roadmap"* caused confusion and a sense of overwhelm in nearly every participant.

> *"Is this for businesses? I just want to run a marathon."*

**What this changed:**
- Full plain-language rewrite of all onboarding copy
- AI reframed as "coach," not productivity framework
- "OKR" → "decide what success looks like"

---

## SLIDE 7 — Key Finding 2: The AI dialogue was the differentiator

**When the conversational AI worked, users responded with genuine enthusiasm.**

The back-and-forth refinement of their goal (not a form — a conversation) made users feel understood.

> *"This is exactly what I needed. Like a personal trainer who actually listens."*

**But:** Any interruption of the conversational flow (dropdowns, toggles, form fields) broke the effect immediately.

**What this changed:**
- No forms inside the goal-setting dialogue — conversation only
- Iterative AI refinement as the core UX mechanic, not a step in a wizard

---

## SLIDE 8 — Key Finding 3: Progress before progress

**Users wanted to feel they were already on the way — before completing a single habit.**

Motivation came from *seeing* the journey mapped out, not from *completing* it.

> *"I want to feel like I'm already on the way before I've done the first thing."*

**What this changed:**
- Visual journey map generated at plan creation (not after first completion)
- Streak counter starts on Day 0
- Micro-celebrations built into every milestone, however small

---

## SLIDE 9 — Design Directions (4 priorities)

| # | Direction | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Plain language rewrite** | Framework jargon = primary onboarding barrier |
| 2 | **Streamlined onboarding** | Free-form goal first; structure emerges progressively |
| 3 | **Conversational AI as core UX** | The dialogue *is* the product — no interruptions |
| 4 | **Visual progress from Day 0** | Motivation tied to seeing the journey, not completing it |

---

## SLIDE 10 — What I Learned

**About AI-product UX:**

The gap between a good AI *concept* and a good AI *experience* is almost entirely a language and framing problem.

The model can be sophisticated. But if the language isn't calibrated to the user's mental model — the intelligence is invisible.

**About product research:**

Concept validation and usability research need to happen together. The discovery interviews told us *what* users needed; the usability tests showed exactly *where* the product failed to provide it.
