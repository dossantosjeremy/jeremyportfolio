# Designing for Customization: Helping HP Customers Configure Their Products

> **One-line hook**: *"Nothing is telling me that the product could be configured."* — HP offered product customization — but customers didn't know it existed until they accidentally discovered it. I researched the discoverability gap and what a better customization experience should communicate.

## Problem & Goal

**User problem:** Customers shopping for HP products needed clear signals that customization was available

![HP laptop customization flow — the CTO experience where changing a spec changed the entire product without users realising.](images/hp-customization-overview.png)
*HP laptop customization flow — the CTO experience where changing a spec changed the entire product without users realising.* — so they could make informed purchase decisions based on their actual configuration needs.

**Research goal:** Understand the discoverability gap

![Methodology — heuristic analysis combined with 72-participant unmoderated usability test across UK age groups.](images/hp-customization-methodology.png)
*Methodology — heuristic analysis combined with 72-participant unmoderated usability test across UK age groups.* for HP's customization options and define what communication, placement, and interaction design would make customization feel like a natural, compelling part of the purchase journey.

## Key Themes

- The entry point to customization was invisible in the current PDP

![Heuristic analysis — usability issues in the existing CTO flow before user testing.](images/hp-customization-heuristic-1.png)
*Heuristic analysis — usability issues in the existing CTO flow before user testing.* — customers progressing normally through the purchase flow had no indication the option existed
- When customization was discoverable, customers responded positively

![Analysis — user preferences across configuration variants, showing strong preference for side-by-side comparison.](images/hp-customization-analysis-1.png)
*Analysis — user preferences across configuration variants, showing strong preference for side-by-side comparison.* — side-by-side comparison of configurations was the most valuable interaction
- The language "configure" was less intuitive than "build your own" or "customise" for mainstream HP customers

---

## Impact

Findings informed the CTO (Configure to Order) product page redesign
