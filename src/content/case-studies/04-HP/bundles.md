# HP Bundles Research

> **One-line hook**: Can selling a laptop and accessories together — as a bundle — improve conversion and customer satisfaction? HP needed to understand how customers perceived and valued bundled offers before investing in the feature at scale.

---

## Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | UX Researcher |
| **Client** | HP — eCommerce (Customer Shopping Insights) |
| **Methods** | Unmoderated usability testing · Interviews |
| **Tools** | UserTesting |

---

## Problem & Goal

**User problem / Business problem:** HP was exploring bundled product offers (laptop + accessories) as a conversion mechanism. Before building and testing bundles in the live environment, the team needed to understand user expectations, perceived value, and potential friction.

**Research goal:** Understand how customers respond to bundled offers

![Prior research context — customer expectations for bundle content, pricing triggers, and preferred discovery moments.](images/hp-bundles-background-1.png)
*Prior research context — customer expectations for bundle content, pricing triggers, and preferred discovery moments.* — whether they perceive value, what content or pricing would make a bundle compelling, and whether bundles integrate naturally into the purchase flow.

---

## Key Research Questions

- How do HP customers currently discover accessories during a laptop purchase journey?

![Card sorting stimuli — bundle types and configurations tested with 84 US participants.](images/hp-bundles-card-sorting-1.png)
*Card sorting stimuli — bundle types and configurations tested with 84 US participants.*
- What does "value" mean in a bundle context — is it primarily about price, convenience, or curation?
- What bundle configurations feel natural vs. arbitrary?
- Where in the purchase journey is a bundle offer most welcome?

![Card sorting results — how participants ranked and categorised bundle options.](images/hp-bundles-card-sorting-2.png)
*Card sorting results — how participants ranked and categorised bundle options.*

## Impact

Research provided the product team with evidence-based bundle design principles

![Key insight — bundle naming clarity: participants couldn't identify certain offers as bundles due to unclear copy and content.](images/hp-bundles-insight-naming.png)
*Key insight — bundle naming clarity: participants couldn't identify certain offers as bundles due to unclear copy and content.* — covering offer positioning, bundle composition, and the narrative framing of value — ahead of the bundles feature development sprint.
