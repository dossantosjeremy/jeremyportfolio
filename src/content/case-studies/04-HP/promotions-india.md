# Helping HP India Customers Shop Through Promotions — $2M Revenue Uplift

> **One-line hook**: A 70% checkout abandonment rate on promotional purchases in India. The call centre was recovering 30% of those sales manually. I identified why — and one design recommendation directly contributed to a $2M projected annual revenue uplift.

## Background & Problem

HP India was experiencing a 70% payment abandonment rate on promotional purchases. Consumers would navigate all the way to checkout — entering their email and phone number — then abandon. The call centre was manually recovering 30% of those lost sales.

The hypothesis: promotions (mainly cashback and exchange offers) were not clear or intuitive, causing consumers to abandon when they encountered unexpected payment requirements they didn't understand.

**My role:** Lead a research programme to diagnose the problem and produce actionable design recommendations.

---

## Research Approach

**Heuristic analysis first** — before running user sessions, I audited the HP India website systematically. The audit produced 14 documented issues.

![Heuristic analysis — 14 documented usability issues identified across the HP India promotions experience, from PDP to checkout.](images/hp-promotions-india-heuristic-analysis.png)
*Heuristic analysis — 14 documented usability issues identified across the HP India promotions experience, from PDP to checkout.* The most critical:

- Promotions were scattered across 8 different locations on the product display page (PDP)
- A "How does financing work?" link in the footer led to a page about printer warranties, not financing
- When a promotion was applicable, the website stated "We currently offer no promotion in this country"
- Bank details (required to claim cashback) were positioned far down the PDP, below the fold, and not visibly connected to the promotion

**Live experience replication in Figma** — the checkout flow existed only in the live HP India environment, which couldn't be shared with research participants. I replicated it in Figma for controlled testing.

**20 unmoderated UserTesting sessions** across three participant segments:

![UserTesting study setup — 20 participants across active consumers, past customers, and cart abandoners on mobile and desktop.](images/hp-promotions-india-usertesting-setup.png)
*UserTesting study setup — 20 participants across active consumers, past customers, and cart abandoners on mobile and desktop.*
- Active consumers actively researching a laptop purchase (who had previously abandoned checkout)
- Past customers who had completed a promotional purchase
- Cart abandoners — consumers who had abandoned before checkout specifically on a promotional product

## Key Findings

**1. Checkout abandonment driven by confusion and payment issues, not price.**

![Affinity diagram — interview data synthesised into themes: abandonment triggers, promotions clarity gaps, and transparency expectations.](images/hp-promotions-india-affinity-diagram.png)
*Affinity diagram — interview data synthesised into themes: abandonment triggers, promotions clarity gaps, and transparency expectations.*

Customers were abandoning because:
- Price changes appeared unexpectedly during checkout
- Promotion details were unclear (which products qualify, how cashback is applied)
- Payment gateway issues specific to promotional products

**2. The promotions flow lacks clarity, guidance, and findability at every stage.**

- Promotions are not findable on the PDP
- The cart provided no guidance on how EMI (equated monthly instalments) interacted with the promotion
- Promotional information during checkout was not self-explanatory

**3. Users expect transparency — and want it early.**

> *"2 or 3 times I had a payment gateway error."*

Users wanted to know: the reason for the sale, full price and promotion details, compatibility with their payment method, and post-sale service details (returns, refunds). **They expected this information before adding to cart, not at checkout.**

---

## Recommendations & Impact

**Key recommendations:**
- Surface bank details (required for cashback) on the product display page, above the fold
- Centralise all promotion information in one location rather than 8 scattered points
- Add guidance in the cart and checkout for EMI options specific to promotional products
- Fix the critical navigation bug: "HP promotions" should lead to promotions, not printer warranties

**Measured impact:**

> Adding bank details to the Product Detail Page contributed to a **projected $2M annual revenue uplift**.

![Research one-pager — key findings and recommendations for rapid stakeholder distribution.](images/hp-promotions-india-one-pager.png)
*Research one-pager — key findings and recommendations for rapid stakeholder distribution.*

This was the direct result of one recommendation from this research — surfacing information that was already available but invisible to users at the moment they needed it to make a purchase decision.

---

## Key Learning

The heuristic analysis was indispensable here. Without the systematic audit first, I would have designed research to explore a vague "promotions problem." The audit narrowed the hypothesis to a specific, testable issue — and the user sessions confirmed it. The $2M finding came from an observation that required no user testing to generate; it required methodical looking.
