# HP Subscriptions Discovery Research

> **One-line hook**: HP was building subscription-based products to stabilise recurring revenue. But customers' mental model of "subscription" was fragmented — and their expectations for managing subscriptions in one place were unmet. I ran discovery research to define what a compelling subscriptions experience would look like.

## Background

HP was investing in subscription-based products — services, ink plans, extended support packages

![Empathy map — mapping what HP subscription customers say, think, feel, and do across their subscription journey.](images/hp-subscriptions-empathy-map.png)
*Empathy map — mapping what HP subscription customers say, think, feel, and do across their subscription journey.* — to achieve consistent monthly revenue alongside hardware sales. Any product providing ongoing services to customers was defined as a subscription for this research.

**User problem:** Customers needed a central management experience for all their HP subscriptions

![Affinity diagram — subscription pain points, expectations, and mental models clustered from interview data.](images/hp-subscriptions-affinity-diagram.png)
*Affinity diagram — subscription pain points, expectations, and mental models clustered from interview data.*, so they could find and control them easily in one place.

**Research goal:** Understand how we can enhance the user experience for subscriptions — making them more visible, manageable, and valuable to HP customers.

---

## Research Questions

- How do customers currently discover and manage HP subscriptions?
- What is their mental model for a "subscription hub" — what do they expect it to contain and where do they expect it to live?
- What would make HP subscriptions feel valuable enough to actively seek out (rather than ignore)?

---

## Approach

**Unmoderated interviews via UserTesting**, designed as a discovery study

![Main insights — the role of free trials and transparent terms in building subscription confidence.](images/hp-subscriptions-insights-1.png)
*Main insights — the role of free trials and transparent terms in building subscription confidence.* to map current behaviour and surface unmet expectations — not to evaluate an existing design.

The study provided the foundational insights for the subscriptions experience design workstream.
