# Incentivising HP Customers to Register Online

> **One-line hook**: *"The time at which I consider registering is when I'm checking out."* — HP had a registration problem. Customers knew what was in it for them, but the timing and framing of the prompt was wrong. I led a rapid research sprint to identify the communication strategies and journey touchpoints that would move the needle on registered user rates.

## Problem & Goal

**User problem:** HP customers needed clearer, better-timed communication about the benefits of registering — so that registration felt like an obvious choice, not an obstacle.

**Business goal:** Increase registered user rates by 5% through better incentive communication and optimised registration prompts.

---

## Research Questions

- What motivates users to register on an eCommerce site?
- What are their expectations for incentives (discounts, exclusive offers, warranty tracking)?
- At which points in the customer journey are users most receptive to a registration prompt?
- What format and content works best for communicating registration benefits?

---

## Approach

**Three-method sprint (January 19–20):**

1. **Heuristic analysis** — audited the current registration flow and surrounding touchpoints against established usability heuristics

## Key Findings

> *"The time at which I consider registering is when I'm checking out."*

> *"I think getting benefits like discounts — 30% off, 20% off, 40% off — for those that register with an account."*

Users were receptive to registration — but expected it at checkout, not on landing pages.

![UX Research Report — thematic analysis findings on what motivates registration and what timing/format works best.](images/hp-registration-report-1.png)
*UX Research Report — thematic analysis findings on what motivates registration and what timing/format works best.* The primary motivator was discounts; secondary motivators included warranty management and exclusive access. Registration prompts mid-journey (between browsing and checkout) were perceived as premature.

## Impact

Findings informed the registration prompt redesign

![UX Research Report — recommendations for registration incentive communication, timing, and copy.](images/hp-registration-report-2.png)
*UX Research Report — recommendations for registration incentive communication, timing, and copy.* — timing, copy, incentive framing, and placement — with a target 5% increase in registered users as the success metric.

**Key learning:** The timing of a registration prompt is as important as its content.

![Research one-pager — key findings and recommendations for rapid stakeholder distribution.](images/hp-registration-one-pager.png)
*Research one-pager — key findings and recommendations for rapid stakeholder distribution.* Asking too early reads as intrusive; asking at the natural commitment moment (checkout) reads as helpful. Users told us exactly when they were willing to register — we just needed to ask.
