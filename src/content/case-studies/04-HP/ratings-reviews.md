# Incentivising HP Customers to Leave Ratings and Reviews

> **One-line hook**: HP's eCommerce store needed more customer reviews — but asking for them at the wrong moment, in the wrong way, was generating low response rates and brand fatigue. I researched what would actually motivate customers to leave a review, and when.

## Problem & Goal

**User problem / Business problem:** HP needed a higher volume of authentic customer reviews to support purchase decisions on the product catalogue. Existing review prompts were generating low engagement.

**Research goal:** Understand what motivates customers to leave ratings and reviews on an eCommerce platform — and design the right prompt, at the right moment, with the right incentive framing.

---

## Research Questions

- What motivates customers to leave a review? (Intrinsic vs. extrinsic)
- What disincentivises review completion? (Effort, timing, trust concerns)
- Which incentive formats are most compelling? (Discounts, loyalty points, social recognition)
- At which post-purchase moment are customers most willing to review?

---

## Key Themes



![Key findings — incentive types ranked by effectiveness at driving review submission behaviour](images/hp-ratings-reviews-findings.png)
*Key findings — incentive types ranked by effectiveness at driving review submission behaviour*HP research surfaced the standard review motivation spectrum — but with nuances specific to the eCommerce context:

- Customers were most willing to review within 7–14 days of receiving a product — after they had genuinely used it, not immediately after delivery
- The most effective incentive framing was social contribution ("help other customers decide") rather than reward-focused ("earn points")
- Review request emails with a single-click star rating embedded in the email outperformed multi-step redirect flows significantly
