# Helping HP Mexico Customers Choose the Right Printer

> **One-line hook**: *"It doesn't help to tell me that this printer is in a range of price between 0 and $1000. In reality it's not telling me anything."* — HP's Mexico shop was asking customers to compare printers without giving them the information that actually drove their decision. I identified the gap between what HP displayed and what customers actually needed.

## Key Finding

The core tension: HP's PLP showed printers by price range

![Preference testing stimuli — PLP design variations tested for information hierarchy and decision support.](images/hp-printer-choice-methodology-2.png)
*Preference testing stimuli — PLP design variations tested for information hierarchy and decision support.*, model number, and marketing tier. Users needed to see ink system information, monthly print volume, and compatibility with their specific use case — none of which were prominently surfaced.

> *"I would put the ink system on the top of the print volume per month. Because that's important for lots of people right?"*

The filtering system (price-based) didn't match the primary decision criteria (use-based and cost-per-page-based) that drove purchase intent.

## Impact

Findings informed the Mexico PLP redesign

![UX Research Report — findings on information hierarchy, trust signals, and recommended design directions.](images/hp-printer-choice-research-report.png)
*UX Research Report — findings on information hierarchy, trust signals, and recommended design directions.* — specifically the hierarchy of printer attributes displayed and the integration of educational content (ink cost calculators, use-case filters) alongside the product catalogue.
