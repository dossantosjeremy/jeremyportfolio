## SLIDE 3 — The Problem

**Current state:** Financing is a delight and surprise experience — not part of the purchasing decision.

**What that means in practice:**
- Customers make their purchase decision based on product value
- They encounter financing options at checkout — *after the decision is made*
- They experience it as a pleasant surprise, not a conversion tool

**Business problem:** HP was investing in a financing feature that wasn't influencing purchase decisions at the moment that mattered.

**Research problem:** No empirical data existed on where financing entered customers' consideration, what they needed to know, or what prevented adoption.

## SLIDE 5 — Approach

**Sequence:**

1. **Competitive analysis** → Understand best-in-class financing integration patterns before speaking to users
2. **Heuristic analysis** → Map current HP.com financing experience issues and form hypotheses
3. **User testing** → Open-ended unmoderated interviews with two segments

**Participant segments:**

| Segment | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Past users** | Bought electronics in past 6 months; previously obtained financing |
| **Active researchers** | Currently researching device purchase; never obtained financing |

**Research questions:**
- What is the impact of financing on the purchase decision?
- What is customers' current understanding of financing?
- What are their attitudes, motivations, concerns, and expectations?

---

## SLIDE 6 — The Central Finding

> **Financing is a delight trigger, not a decision driver.**

Customers decide to buy based on product value. They then discover financing as a pleasant surprise that makes them feel better about the decision they already made.

**This reframed everything:**

| Old assumption | New understanding |
|---|---|
| Financing helps people decide to buy | Financing helps people feel confident after deciding to buy |
| Optimize the checkout financing module | Surface financing earlier in the journey — at discovery and comparison stages |
| One financing message for all | Differentiate consumer vs. SMB financing by motivation and need |

**Competitive insight:** Best-in-class platforms surface financing at 3 stages (discovery, comparison, commitment). HP was only surfacing it at commitment.

---

## SLIDE 7 — E2E Touchpoint Architecture

Financing needs to appear across the full customer journey:

| Journey Stage | Financing Role |
|---|---|
| Product discovery (category) | Monthly payment as a *price framing* tool |
| Product comparison | Financing eligibility as a *comparative filter* |
| Product detail page | Calculator + eligibility indicator |
| Cart | Confirmation and clarity — *not* first introduction |
| Post-purchase | Loyalty and repeat purchase framing |

## SLIDE 9 — Learnings

**What I'd repeat:** Leading with competitive analysis before user research. It gave me domain vocabulary and reference patterns that made user sessions far richer — I could probe with specificity, not just discover basics.

**What I'd improve:** Add a moderated component alongside unmoderated testing. The open-ended design was valuable, but I missed real-time probing of unexpected responses on a project with this level of strategic scope.

**Key lesson:** When research contradicts an existing assumption (financing as decision driver vs. delight trigger), you need both competitive data AND user data to make the insight adoptable — not just interesting.
