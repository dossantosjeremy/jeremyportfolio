# Improving HP India's Login Experience to Reduce Checkout Abandonment

> **One-line hook**: *"It becomes very easy to access the next time you land up on the site while you don't have to put your passwords again and again."* — Login friction was driving checkout abandonment on HP.com India. A cumbersome authentication flow was losing customers at the final step. I led the research that defined what a better experience should look like.

## Problem & Goal

**User problem:** Users experienced friction and checkout abandonment

**Business goal:** Streamline the authentication process, making it more intuitive and less intrusive, minimising checkout abandonment.

**Multi-source approach:**

**Discovery phase:** Explored authentication experiences and mental models

**Evaluation phase:** Tested specific design directions against user expectations.

---

## Key Findings

- Streamlined login — particularly returning user recognition — was the primary driver of satisfaction
- Users preferred minimal personal data collection at the account creation stage
- Familiar authentication methods (Google/Gmail sign-in) were strongly preferred over HP-specific password flows
- The current login prompt — appearing mid-checkout — was perceived as an interruption rather than a natural step

---

## Recommendations

1. Implement social login (Google/Gmail) as the primary authentication option
2. Reduce mandatory data collection at signup — progressive profiling post-checkout
3. Move the login prompt to a point in the journey where it feels like a natural step, not an interruption
4. Surface returning-user recognition earlier to enable one-tap authentication
