# Helping HP Customers Return Their Orders (UK)

> **One-line hook**: *"I think the most important is how quick the return can be processed and how efficient it is."* — 24 UK customers. 12 who had returned a product before. 12 who never had. Their combined mental models defined what a trustworthy return experience should feel like — and where HP's fell short.

## Problem & Goal

**User problem:** Customers need an intuitive and efficient process to manage post-purchase requests — particularly returns — minimising the need to call or chat with customer service.

**Business goal:** Improve customer satisfaction on post-purchase journeys and reduce contact centre load driven by return-related queries.

---

## Research Design

**24 unmoderated sessions** with a carefully constructed participant matrix:

![HP post-sales return flow — the experience evaluated across 24 UK participants.](images/hp-returns-uk-overview.png)
*HP post-sales return flow — the experience evaluated across 24 UK participants.*
- 12 active HP customers + 12 past HP customers
- 12 mobile + 12 desktop participants
- 6 participants per age range (18–24, 25–34, 35–44, 45+)
- 12 male, 12 female
- 12 who had previously returned an online purchase + 12 who had never done so

The split between experienced returners and first-time returners was critical

![UserTesting setup — 24 UK participants carefully split by return experience, device type, age, and gender.](images/hp-returns-uk-usertesting.png)
*UserTesting setup — 24 UK participants carefully split by return experience, device type, age, and gender.*: it allowed us to separate the mental model that people *bring* to the return flow from the friction they encounter *within* it.

---

## Key Questions

- What are customers' expectations for a return experience, before they start the process?
- Where does the current HP return flow fail those expectations?
- What differences exist between mobile and desktop experiences?
- How does prior return experience shape tolerance for friction?

---

## Impact

Findings and UX guidelines were developed collaboratively with the Customer Shopping Insights team.

![UX Research Report — findings and UX guidelines for optimising the HP post-sales return experience.](images/hp-returns-uk-research-report.png)
*UX Research Report — findings and UX guidelines for optimising the HP post-sales return experience.* Recommendations addressed the structural gaps between customer expectations (fast, self-service, status-visible) and the actual return experience.

> *"I think also you would need to know the amount of time that you would have to return it."* — participant quote surfaced in research

This study contributed to the contact centre load reduction programme
